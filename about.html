Our Story

About Bethesda

A warm, friendly, caring and loving church — a place of healing in Houghton, Johannesburg, since 1904.

Est. 1904

A Century of Faith & Service

Founded in 1904 as Berea Methodist Church, Bethesda Methodist Mission originally stood at the corner of Joe Slovo Drive and Alexander Street in Berea, Johannesburg. In 1952, the municipality proposed the expropriation of the church property for road development, leading to the congregation's relocation to Houghton in 1962, where the current church building was officially opened.

The democratic transition of the 1990s marked a significant turning point, as the congregation became increasingly diverse and representative of the surrounding communities. Worship services evolved to become more inclusive, incorporating vernacular worship and broader community participation.

A major milestone was the transformation from Berea Methodist Church to Bethesda Methodist Mission. The name "Bethesda", meaning place of healing, reflected a renewed vision of outreach, restoration, and community impact. The church also introduced a cell-group ministry model, strengthening fellowship and spiritual growth within smaller community groups. In 2004, the church celebrated its centenary by launching a mission fund to support outreach initiatives.

In recent years, Bethesda embraced digital ministry by launching online worship services through Facebook and YouTube, expanding the church's reach beyond physical gatherings.

Today, Bethesda Methodist Mission serves a congregation of approximately 850 members alongside a growing online community, committed to worship, discipleship, outreach, and community care.

Our flagship feeding scheme, established in 2006, provides weekly meals to more than 400 people from surrounding communities.
